PART 2: DESIGN (Answer ALL questions)

1. What overall style will best represent you (minimalist, creative, professional, etc.)?
More Minimalist, a bit professional.
2. What color scheme will you use and why?
Light colored backgrounds, with darker clean text to call attention towards the elements.
3. What fonts will you use for headings and body text?
I decided on Manrope, a googleAPI font that looks better than arial to me.
4. How will your design reflect your personality or field?
I like clean layouts for the most part, with more succinct and straight to the point action, 
I believe that reflects my personality, and the way I prefer to do my work.
5. What layout will your homepage follow?
A short intro, some bullet points about what are the main skills I embody, and then a carousel for the images of my 
featured project.
6. How will you organize project sections visually?
Using Cards, and aligning things above each other, so that there is coherency between elements.
7. Will the site be mobile-friendly? How will you ensure responsiveness?
No, not generally.
8. What visual hierarchy will guide visitors?
The navbar at the top will give an indication about which pages have more outside information, and which pages are more an extension
of myself.
9. How will consistency be maintained across pages?
Keeping my css style applied across each page, and using section and div classes generally with the same structure
for groups of elements, so that flex and alignment can be used in the same ways across the entire site.
10. How will accessibility be considered (contrast, font size, readability)?
sans-serif font will be used, and in places where text is not as readable, potentially I will have a contrasting background behind the text.
Font size should be big enough for even a very large screen to see text. 
11. Will you use icons, images, or illustrations? Why?
Images of my app from GitHub, showing some of the functionality, favicon icon for my personal touch, 
pngs of technologies/tools I use and potentially would like to get into , all to show visually some 
variety in my intentions.
12. What portfolio websites inspired your design?
Not really much, more Figma and messing with templates got me to my design.

PART 3: INTERACTIVITY (Answer ALL questions)

1. What interactive elements will your site include (navigation menus, buttons, forms)?
Navigation Menu, with hover reacting buttons , and a working dropdown, as well as a working carousel, and a contact form 
at the bottom.
2. Will your site include a contact form? How will it work?
It will include a contact form that should have a space for an email to be left, and a subject
, also there should be my email link in the footer if one would like to directly contact me.
3. What JavaScript features will you implement?
I will implement the carousel manually, and potentially another interactive Photo element.
4. How will users receive feedback from interactions?
Hovering over images or buttons will validate that they are interacting with the website.
Clicking buttons will bring them to different sectors of the website, or different elements. Opening accordions 
will react as it should.
5. How does interactivity improve the user experience?
Interactivity lets the user know that their actions are having an effect on the website,
and makes the user feel that the website is not an immovable picture, but rather something
that is curated to them, or their experiences , which retains user interest more.
